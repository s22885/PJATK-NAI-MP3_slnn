McTavish was tried for the murder in 1974 of an 80-year-old patient, Elizabeth Lyon,[3][4] and assaulting three other patients by giving them illegal injections.[5] One victim was found after tests to have an inexplicable quantity of pethidine in their system, whilst the murder victim had been injected with soluble insulin.[2] Colleagues of McTavish told the court how they had witnessed her inject a patient with an entirely unnecessary dose of phenobarbitone and then make no record of the injection, and that she had said at the time, "Doctor likes them to go quietly".[2] Multiple doctors testified that McTavish often gave patients injections without recording these events in the patients' case notes.[2]
